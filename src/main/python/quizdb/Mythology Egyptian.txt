During the Middle Kingdom, this deity was merged with the Abydosian god Khenti-Amentiu, taking on the epithet “Foremost of the Westerners.” This deity was the eldest son of the earth god Geb and the sky goddess Nut, and is often depicted wearing an atef crown with a crook and (*) flail in his hands. After being separated into fourteen pieces by his brother Set, this god’s wife, assisted by her sister Nephthys, tracked down all remaining pieces before binding them back together. For 10 points, name this husband of Isis, the Egyptian god of the Underworld.
